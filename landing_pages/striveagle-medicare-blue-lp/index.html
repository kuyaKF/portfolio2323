What is Medicare?
Medicare provides affordable medical care to seniors (age 65 or older) and those with disabilities, covering hospital stays, doctor visits, and prescription drugs.
It's a crucial investment in your health, making it easier to take control of your well-being without worrying about the high costs of medical care.
Learn About the benefits & what it covers
Hospital insurance
Part A, also known as Medicare Part A, provides coverage for hospital stays, skilled nursing facility care, hospice care, and certain types of home health care.
medical insurance
Part B, which is also referred to as Medicare Part B, covers specific medical services provided by doctors, outpatient care, medical supplies, and preventive services.
Prescription insurance
Part D, also known as Medicare Part D, is designed to assist with the cost of prescription drugs, including many recommended vaccines and shots.

what is medicare and who is eligible for it?
Medicare covers medical services for those aged 65 and over, people with permanent kidney failure or Lou Gehrig's disease, and those with certain disabilities who have been receiving Social Security Disability Insurance for 24 months. It's vital healthcare coverage for those who need it.
