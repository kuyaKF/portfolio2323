Why Go Solar?

Investing in solar panels guarantees both short-term and long-term financial security.

They start saving you money on your energy bills immediately after installation while supplying you with clean, sustainable power. Plus, if you produce more energy than you use on sunny days, you can sell the excess back to your local grid for credits.

Additionally, going solar not only benefits the environment but also offers tax benefits: until 2032, you can deduct 30% of your solar purchase and installation costs, providing extra savings.

Are Solar Panels Expensive?

Solar or PV panels might appear pricey at first, but there's encouraging news! Providers are dedicated to making clean energy accessible to all.

While the average cost of a PV system falls between $3,000 and $15,000, you'll find a variety of flexible payment options emerging. These options include monthly or bi-annual installments, leasing arrangements, and partial financing programs.

From Sunlight to Lightbulb

Solar panels are mounted on a flat surface, preferably with a clear view of the sky.

Solar panels are composed of 2 layers of a semiconductor (usually silicon), glass, and wire for conduction

A neutral silicon atom contains 14 positively charged protons at its core and 14 negatively charged electrons in shells around it.

Light particles from the sun (photons) strike the silicon atom.

The energy from the photons knock electrons loose from the outer shell. When an electron moves, it emits electric energy.

This process of loose, moving electrons is repeated in sequence, so that electrons move around the solar cells and create an electric current.

The direct electrical current from the electrons is converted into an alternating current via an inverter, which can be connected to your homeâ€™s power supply.

The solar panels have successfully converted sunlight into clean, green energy for your home.
